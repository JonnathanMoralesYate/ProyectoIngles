Panela History

Beginnings

Panela production in Tolima was consolidated during the 18th and 19th centuries, becoming a fundamental economic activity for the region. The sugar mills were established mainly in areas with warm and temperate climates, such as the municipalities of Alvarado, Chaparral, Ibagué and Mariquita.

Socioeconomic

Panela has become a staple in the diet of the people of Tolima and an important source of income for farmers. The sugarcane industry has historically been a generator of rural employment and a factor of cultural roots.

Development

The sugarcane industry in Tolima underwent a gradual modernization. New sugarcane varieties were introduced and production techniques were improved. Also, programs were implemented to improve quality and production efficiency. Panela gained recognition as a natural and healthy product.

Main Producers in Tolima

The major producers in Tolima in the manufacture of panela are Mariquita, Fresno, Alvarado and Ibague.

Panela Process

Raw Material Collection

Cutting and transportation of sugar cane to the sugar mill.

Extraction

Milling of the cane to obtain the juice or guarapo.

Pre-cleaning

Removal of coarse impurities from the juice.

Clarification

Addition of natural substances such as balsam or cadillo to eliminate fine impurities.

Evaporation

Heating of the juice in kettles to reduce the water content.

Concentration

Evaporation continues until the panela point is reached.

Stitching

Determination of the exact crystallization point.

Beating

Stirring to cool and aerate the honey.

Molding

Pouring into molds to shape the panela.

Packaging

Once cold, it is packed for distribution.

Implementation

Environment

The taste of sugarcane with eco-friendly process, have built 5 eco-efficient paneleras stoves, in the village of Cabaña Baja, rural area of Mariquita in northern Tolima. The implementation of this project results in the benefit of a cleaner production process, since the forest is no longer used for firewood as fuel for the stove, since it is replaced by sugarcane bagasse for combustion. In addition, energy efficiency improves production in less time, reduces labor costs and improves product quality.

Tourism

In search of generating new opportunities to make the north of Tolima more visible, the idea is to create a productive tourism corridor that teaches about the panel process through a tour of its processes in the midst of the impressive landscapes of the north of Tolima in the Totumo de Anzoátegui trail in Anzoátegui.
